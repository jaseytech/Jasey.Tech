Safety First

I wanted to touch upon a few scams going around. These aren’t scams that just impact Salesforce. They impact thousands around the world, and even more so when you consider the family and friends who are impacted when a loved one gets scammed.

The first scam is called a refund scam. First of all, please be advised that Microsoft, Apple, or any other corporation will never ever call you to offer you a refund out of the blue. They most definitely will NOT require you to log in to your bank account while they connect to your computer remotely.

If anyone calls you out of the blue, offers you a refund, asks to connect to your computer, asks if you use Google Chrome, and/or asks you to log in your bank while they black out the screen or distract you, run for the hills. Don’t let them on your computer. Don’t log in to your bank. Hang up on them and block or report the number.

If you did happen to let them on your computer and log in, never ever trust them when they say they’ve “accidentally” refunded more than you were due. They will claim they will lose their job or even may threaten you with police action if you don’t return the money. They will likely ask you to send them the cash in the mail or through gift cards. A legitimate company will never ask you to do this.

What they have done is simply edit the HTML (basically the site’s appearance) to make it LOOK like they’ve sent you money using Google Chrome’s development tool feature. In actuality, they haven’t sent you any money! They’ve only made it look like they have using this tool or transferring money between your own accounts. For example, if they say you’re due $200, they will “accidentally” send you $2000– or make it look like they have. Don’t trust this! They just want you to give them gift cards so they can sell them online and be virtually untraceable.

If you think you may have fallen for the scam, be sure to contact your bank and a trustworthy loved one. They will help determine your next steps. Just remember, if something sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

Please stay tuned to this site, as I will be releasing more in-depth information on both this scam and others like it regularly.

Quick Update:

I’m taking my next Salesforce Certification exam on my birthday, January 7th. I figured that there would be no better way to celebrate than to use the free voucher I won and go out there and get a new cert.

This time, I’m going for the admin certification. This is a lot of people’s first certification. This is, in part, because several career paths require admin knowledge. It’s also just incredibly useful for building a fundamental knowledge for the platform. Had I not wanted the Developer title so bad, I would have gone for it first myself.

In fact, when I first really got interested in working in Salesforce in 2012, I almost got the admin certification right away. My boyfriend at the time was a developer, and I had absolutely no interest in development. I was interested, however, in Salesforce, so the admin path seemed like a perfect fit!

I remember the first time he linked me to the Salesforce workbook and how it felt to be tackling those projects head on. I would feel so accomplished each time I built an app or got security settings just right. It’s almost a nostalgic feeling now that I’ve been working in the ecosystem for a few years.

After getting my admin cert, I plan on helping Scotty prepare for his better. Having the firsthand experience of sitting down and taking it will definitely help me to better help him prepare. Once we get him that first cert, we’ll tackle the consultant certs! It’s an amazing first step towards the rest of his life as a successful Salesforce professional, so I want to make sure I’m able to help as best I can.

I hope you’re all having an amazing 2020 so far. I believe in you!

Salesforce Community

Almost immediately after I started posting about my journey here, a few members of the Salesforce community began following me on either Twitter or LinkedIn. To me that speaks volumes about the support and care this community has for its members. They have shown belief in me and it’s a good feeling.

You may have noticed while on Trailhead or other Salesforce pages/posts that the word Ohana is used. And to quote Lilo & Stitch, Ohana means family. It truly does feel like a family. One who welcomes you, supports you, and guides you throughout your journey.

We all came to Salesforce for various reasons but in it, found a place where we belong, where our talents can shine. I’ve said it in the past but we are truly in this together.
